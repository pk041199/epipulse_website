Join our team of dedicated volunteers making a difference in public health. Learn about volunteer opportunities, roles, and how to get involved.

Volunteer with EpiPulse

EpiPulse is a public health initiative committed to evidence-based action. We rely on passionate volunteers to help us achieve our mission of improving health outcomes through education, research, and community engagement.

Why Volunteer with Us?

  • Make a Real Impact: Contribute to meaningful public health projects that directly benefit communities
  • Skill Development: Gain experience in public health, research, communication, and project management
  • Networking: Connect with like-minded professionals and experts in the field
  • Flexible Commitment: Choose roles that fit your schedule and interests
  • Recognition: Get certificates and public acknowledgment for your contributions

Current Volunteer Opportunities

We have several volunteer positions available. Each role has specific requirements, expectations, and benefits. Please review the details below and apply if you meet the eligibility criteria.

1. Content Creator

Role Description: Create educational content, blog posts, social media materials, and resources for our website and platforms.

Expectations: - Produce 2-4 pieces of content per month - Research and fact-check information - Collaborate with the team on content strategy - Meet deadlines and quality standards

Benefits: - Writing portfolio development - Public health knowledge enhancement - Byline credits on published work - Certificate of contribution

Eligibility Criteria: - Strong writing and communication skills - Interest in public health topics - Access to computer and internet - Basic knowledge of public health concepts (preferred)

2. Idea Contributor & Strategist

Role Description: Provide innovative ideas for projects, campaigns, and initiatives. Help develop strategies for program implementation.

Expectations: - Attend monthly brainstorming sessions - Submit 1-2 ideas per month - Participate in strategy discussions - Help refine and implement selected ideas

Benefits: - Strategic thinking skills development - Leadership experience - Networking with team members - Certificate of contribution

Eligibility Criteria: - Creative thinking and problem-solving skills - Interest in public health innovation - Good communication skills - Experience in project planning (preferred)

3. Activity Coordinator

Role Description: Organize and conduct community health activities, workshops, and awareness campaigns.

Expectations: - Plan and execute 1-2 activities per quarter - Coordinate with local partners and stakeholders - Manage logistics and participant engagement - Report on activity outcomes

Benefits: - Event management experience - Community engagement skills - Leadership opportunities - Certificate of contribution

Eligibility Criteria: - Organizational and coordination skills - Experience with community work (preferred) - Good interpersonal skills - Access to local resources/networks

4. IEC Materials Developer

Role Description: Design and create Information, Education, and Communication (IEC) materials including posters, infographics, videos, and presentations.

Expectations: - Create 2-3 IEC materials per month - Use design software (Canva, Adobe Suite, etc.) - Ensure materials are culturally appropriate and accessible - Collaborate with content creators and health experts

Benefits: - Graphic design and multimedia skills - Portfolio development - Creative expression opportunities - Certificate of contribution

Eligibility Criteria: - Basic design skills or willingness to learn - Access to design software - Understanding of health communication principles - Attention to detail

5. Online OPD Consultant

Role Description: Provide online consultations for patients through our telehealth platform. Guide patients on public health issues and basic medical advice.

Expectations: - Conduct 4-8 consultation sessions per week - Maintain detailed consultation logs - Follow ethical guidelines and privacy standards - Refer complex cases to appropriate specialists

Benefits: - Clinical experience and skill enhancement - Professional development in telehealth - Networking with healthcare professionals - Certificate of contribution and CME credits (if applicable)

Eligibility Criteria: - Qualified healthcare professional (MBBS, Nursing, etc.) - Valid medical license - Experience in patient consultation - Comfortable with digital tools and online platforms

6. OPD Channel Manager

Role Description: Manage online OPD appointments, coordinate consultation schedules, and maintain patient records.

Expectations: - Schedule and manage appointments - Coordinate with consultants and patients - Maintain accurate logs and records - Handle technical issues with the platform

Benefits: - Administrative and coordination skills - Experience in healthcare management - Understanding of telehealth operations - Certificate of contribution

Eligibility Criteria: - Strong organizational skills - Experience with scheduling software - Good communication skills - Basic understanding of healthcare processes

Online OPD Platform

We’re launching our online OPD service using Google Meet for video consultations. Here’s how it works:

For Patients:

  1. Book Appointment: Use our online booking system to schedule a consultation
  2. Receive Link: Get a personalized Google Meet link via email/SMS
  3. Join Consultation: Connect at the scheduled time for your virtual appointment
  4. Get Prescription: Receive digital prescriptions and follow-up instructions

For Consultants:

  1. Check Schedule: View your assigned consultation slots
  2. Join Meeting: Use the provided Google Meet link
  3. Consult: Provide professional medical advice
  4. Document: Maintain detailed consultation logs

Technical Setup:

  • We’re using a dedicated Google account for NGO activities
  • Google Meet links are embedded in our website for seamless access
  • Appointments are managed through an integrated calendar system

Prescription Management

For digital prescriptions, we recommend the following approach:

Option 1: Digital Prescription Templates

  • Use standardized templates in Google Docs or Microsoft Word
  • Include patient details, diagnosis, medications, and instructions
  • Email prescriptions to patients with digital signatures

Option 2: Prescription Software Integration

  • Consider free/open-source prescription software
  • Integrate with our website for direct prescription generation
  • Ensure compliance with local medical regulations

Option 3: Partner with Telemedicine Platforms

  • Explore partnerships with established telemedicine services
  • Use their prescription management systems
  • Maintain data privacy and security

We’re currently evaluating the best option that complies with Indian medical regulations and our NGO’s capabilities.

How to Apply

Interested in volunteering? Here’s how to get started:

  1. Review Opportunities: Read the role descriptions above
  2. Prepare Application: Send us your resume/CV and a brief cover letter
  3. Contact Us: Email volunteer@epipulse.org with your application
  4. Interview: We’ll schedule a quick chat to discuss your interests
  5. Orientation: Join our volunteer orientation session
  6. Get Started: Begin contributing to our mission!

Volunteer Guidelines

  • Commitment: We expect consistent participation based on your agreed schedule
  • Confidentiality: All patient and organizational information must be kept confidential
  • Ethics: Follow ethical guidelines in all interactions
  • Communication: Regular updates and feedback are essential
  • Professionalism: Maintain professional conduct in all volunteer activities
